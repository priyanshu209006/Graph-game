Marbleslides — Free Online Graphing Calculator Game

Marbleslides is a free, interactive graphing calculator game where you type mathematical equations, plot curves on a coordinate plane, launch marbles with realistic physics, and collect stars to score points. Whether you're a student practicing algebra, a teacher looking for engaging math activities, or a math enthusiast who loves graphing — Marbleslides makes learning functions fun.

Supported Equation Types

Plot a wide range of mathematical functions and curves:

  • Linear equations — y = mx + b, slope-intercept form, horizontal and vertical lines
  • Quadratic & polynomial functions — y = x², y = x³, parabolas, cubics
  • Trigonometric functions — sin(x), cos(x), tan(x) with amplitude, frequency, and phase shifts
  • Exponential & logarithmic — y = e^x, y = ln(x), y = log(x), exponential growth and decay
  • Square root & absolute value — y = sqrt(x), y = abs(x)
  • Polar equations — r = f(θ), spirals, roses, cardioids, limaçons
  • Implicit curves — circles (x² + y² = r²), ellipses, conic sections
  • Piecewise functions — domain-restricted equations with conditions like y = x {x > 0}

Five Game Modes

  • Classic — Balanced puzzles with 4–6 stars and up to 10 equations
  • Precision — Win with only 4 equations — requires clean, intentional graphing
  • Blitz — 75-second timed sprint — plot fast and beat the clock
  • Zen — Unlimited equations and no pressure — perfect for experimenting
  • Daily — One shared puzzle each day with streak tracking across sessions

How to Play

  1. Type an equation in the input field (e.g., y = 0.5x + 2)
  2. Click Plot to draw the curve on the coordinate plane
  3. Set the marble launch position using the x,y coordinate controls
  4. Click Launch Marbles to release them with gravity and physics
  5. Collect all stars to complete the puzzle and earn your score

Marbleslides works on desktop, tablet, and mobile browsers. No download or account required. Browse equation examples to learn the syntax for lines, parabolas, trig functions, polar equations, piecewise functions, and more.
